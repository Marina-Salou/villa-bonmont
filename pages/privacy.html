Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 19, 2026

1. Introduction

Villa Bonmont Terres Noves ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") operates the website villabonmont.com. This page informs you of our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of personal data when you use our Service and the choices you have associated with that data.

We use your data to provide and improve the Service. By using the Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. This Privacy Policy is aligned with GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) and other applicable data protection laws.

2. Definitions

  • Service: The website and any associated applications, including content and features.
  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable person.
  • Processing: Any operation performed on data (e.g., collection, use, storage).
  • Controller: The entity determining the purposes and means of processing (Villa Bonmont).
  • Data Subject: The individual to whom personal data relates (you).

3. Information Collection & Use

3.1 Information We Collect

We collect information in the following ways:

  • Directly Provided: When you fill out forms, contact us, or make inquiries
  • Automatically Collected: Through cookies, pixels, and analytics tools
  • From Third Parties: From payment processors, Airbnb, or social media platforms

3.2 Types of Data Collected

  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Reservation dates and preferences
  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referral source
  • Device type (mobile, tablet, desktop)

3.3 Purpose of Data Processing

We process your data for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain the Service
  • To process inquiries and bookings
  • To communicate with you about your reservation
  • To improve website usability and performance
  • To comply with legal obligations
  • To prevent fraud and ensure security
  • To send optional marketing communications (with consent)

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under GDPR, we process your data based on the following legal grounds:

  • Consent: When you have explicitly consented to processing
  • Contract: When processing is necessary to fulfill a reservation agreement
  • Legal Obligation: When we must process data to comply with law
  • Legitimate Interest: To improve services and prevent fraud

5. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience:

  • Technical Cookies: Required for site functionality (no consent needed)
  • Analytics Cookies: To understand how you use the site (consent required)
  • Preference Cookies: To remember your choices

You can control cookies through the cookie consent banner and your browser settings.

6. Analytics

We use Google Analytics 4 with anonymized IP addresses and privacy-enhanced configuration. Analytics cookies are only loaded if you have consented. You can opt-out at any time via the cookie settings.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy:

  • Reservation Data: Retained for 5 years for accounting and legal purposes
  • Contact Information: Retained until you request deletion
  • Analytics Data: Automatically deleted after 14 months
  • Cookies: Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser

8. Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell or share your personal data. We may share data with:

  • Airbnb: For reservation processing and hosting
  • Payment Processors: To process payments securely
  • Service Providers: Website hosting, analytics, and communication tools
  • Legal Authorities: When required by law

All third parties are bound by data protection agreements and may only process data as directed.

9. Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Obtain a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to Restrict: Limit how we process your data
  • Right to Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Object: Opt-out of marketing communications
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: Contact your local data protection authority

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the details provided below.

10. International Data Transfers

Your data may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that differ from your home country. By using the Service, you consent to the transfer of your information to countries outside your country of residence. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for any transfers, including standard contractual clauses.

11. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • HTTPS/SSL encryption for all data transmission
  • Secure password protection
  • Regular security audits
  • Limited access to personal data

However, no security system is impenetrable. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

12. Children's Privacy

Our Service is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that a child has provided us with personal information, we will delete such information and terminate the child's account.

13. Contact Information

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, or wish to exercise your rights, please contact us:

  • Email: privacy@villabonmont.com
  • Address: Bonmont Terres Noves, Castelldefels, Catalunya 08348, Spain
  • Response Time: We will respond to your request within 30 days

14. Data Protection Officer

For GDPR-related inquiries, you may contact our Data Protection Officer:

  • Email: dpo@villabonmont.com

15. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. The "Last updated" date indicates when this policy was last revised. Your continued use of the Service after changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.

16. Complaint Rights

If you believe our processing of your personal data violates your rights, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. For residents of the EU:

  • Spain: Spanish Data Protection Authority (AEPD) - www.aepd.es
  • Other EU countries: Your national data protection authority
